Privacy Policy

We built OTRUST so you don't have to trust us. Zero-knowledge architecture means your data stays yours.

Last updated: December 31, 2025

TL;DR – The Important Bits

Timestamp: We never see your content. Only a cryptographic hash (fingerprint) is transmitted.

Signed: Your choice. Zero-knowledge or temporary upload (encrypted, auto-deleted 1-12h).

Proof: Local processing. Your ID and photos never leave your browser. Only cryptographic proof is stored.

No accounts or tracking. No cookies, no analytics profiles, no data selling.

Fully open source. Audit every line of code yourself.

Data Collection – Timestamp Service

We Never Collect

  • Your original files or documents
  • Text content, images, or media
  • Personal information or names
  • Email addresses (unless you contact us)
  • Tracking cookies or fingerprints
  • Browsing history or behavior
  • Device identifiers

✓ We Do Store

  • SHA-256 hash of your content
  • Timestamp of submission
  • OpenTimestamps proof data
  • Optional filename (if provided)
  • Source identifier (web, cli, etc.)
  • IP for rate limiting (not stored)

Data Collection – Signed Service

OTRUST Signed offers two privacy modes:

✓ Zero-Knowledge Mode

  • You share document directly with signers
  • Only document hash stored on our servers
  • Same privacy as Timestamp service
  • Signer email for invitation delivery only

✓ Temporary Upload Mode

  • Document encrypted at rest (AES-256)
  • Auto-deleted after 1, 6, or 12 hours
  • Access via secure token only
  • Cryptographic proof of deletion provided

Signature Data

For both modes, we store: document hash, signer identifiers (name/email as provided), cryptographic signatures, and timestamps. Signature records are permanent to enable future verification.

Data Collection – Proof Service

OTRUST Proof creates zero-knowledge identity proofs using ZK-SNARKs:

We Never Receive

  • Your ID document images
  • Your selfie or photos
  • Personal data (name, DOB, address)
  • Your actual ID number
  • Biometric data
  • Face recognition templates

✓ We Do Store

  • ZK-SNARK proof (cryptographic commitment)
  • Public signals from the proof
  • One-way hash of ID number (for Sybil resistance)
  • Proof type and creation timestamp
  • Verification key ID

How Zero-Knowledge Works

All sensitive processing happens in your browser using JavaScript. Your ID is scanned by Tesseract.js (local OCR), face matching uses face-api.js (local models), and the ZK-SNARK proof is generated by snarkjs. Only the final cryptographic proof – which reveals nothing about your actual data – is sent to our servers.

Sybil Resistance

To ensure one proof per person, we hash your ID number (e.g., Swedish personnummer) with a secret salt. This produces a unique identifier that cannot be reversed to reveal your actual ID. If you try to create a second proof, the system will detect the duplicate hash without ever knowing your real ID number.

What is a Hash?

A SHA-256 hash is a one-way mathematical fingerprint. It's a 64-character string that uniquely identifies your content, but it's mathematically impossible to reverse. You cannot reconstruct a document from its hash – not even with unlimited computing power.

Data Details

Why permanent? Timestamps must be verifiable years or decades later. Deleting records would make verification impossible.

Platform-Specific Details

Web Tool & Browser Extension

Hashing happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your file is never uploaded – it's read locally, hashed locally, and only the hash is sent to our API. You can verify this in your browser's network tab.

ChatGPT Integration

When using the OTRUST GPT, ChatGPT's Code Interpreter computes the hash. Only the hash is sent to OTRUST. Note: OpenAI has access to your full conversation under their privacy policy – we do not.

CLI Tool

The command-line tool runs entirely on your machine. Hashing is done locally using Node.js crypto. Only the hash is transmitted to the API.

Your Rights

Since we don't collect personal data, traditional GDPR rights work differently:

  • Right to Access: Timestamp records are public. Anyone can look up a hash.
  • Right to Deletion: Timestamps are permanent by design – deletion would defeat the purpose.
  • Right to Verification: You can verify any timestamp independently using OpenTimestamps clients. No trust in OTRUST required.
  • Right to Portability: Download your .ots proof files anytime. They work with any OTS client.
  • Right to Object: Contact us with concerns about specific records.
